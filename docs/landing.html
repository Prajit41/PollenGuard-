🌼 Main Causes

Pollen allergies are triggered by tiny particles released by trees, grasses, and weeds. Common triggers include:

  • Tree pollen (spring)
  • Grass pollen (late spring/summer)
  • Weed pollen (fall)
  • Mold spores (year-round in warm climates)

⚠️ Severity Levels

Pollen counts can vary throughout the day and season:

  • Low: 0-14 grains/m³ - Minimal symptoms
  • Moderate: 15-89 grains/m³ - Mild to moderate symptoms
  • High: 90-1,499 grains/m³ - Symptoms likely
  • Very High: 1,500+ grains/m³ - Severe symptoms expected

💊 Common Medications

Several medications can help manage symptoms:

  • Antihistamines: Relieve sneezing, itching, runny nose
  • Decongestants: Reduce nasal congestion
  • Nasal sprays: Reduce inflammation and congestion
  • Eye drops: Relieve itchy, watery eyes

🛡️ Prevention Tips

  • Check daily pollen forecasts
  • Keep windows closed during high pollen counts
  • Shower after being outdoors
  • Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
  • Wear sunglasses to protect your eyes
  • Change clothes after being outside
