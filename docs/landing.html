What Are Pollen Allergies?

Pollen allergies, also known as hay fever or allergic rhinitis, occur when your immune system overreacts to pollen particles in the air.

Key Facts:

  • Affects 30% of adults and 40% of children
  • Most common in spring, summer, and fall
  • Symptoms include sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes

Common Pollen Types

🌳

Trees

Oak, birch, cedar, and maple trees release pollen in early spring.

🌾

Grasses

Common in late spring and summer, including ryegrass and timothy grass.

🌿

Weeds

Ragweed is the most common, peaking in late summer and fall.

Prevention Strategies

🌡️ Monitor Pollen Counts

Check daily forecasts and stay indoors when counts are high.

🛌 Nighttime Routine

Shower before bed to remove pollen from hair and skin.

🏡 Home Environment

Keep windows closed and use HEPA filters in your home.
